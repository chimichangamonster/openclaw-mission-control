Client Onboarding
Preparation Checklist

VantageClaw AI Business Platform

Please review and prepare the following items before your onboarding session.

Having this information ready lets us configure your organization accurately and get you operational faster. Most items take 5-10 minutes to gather. Don't worry if you don't have everything — we'll work through gaps together.

1 Cost Code Structure

  • Full list of cost codes with descriptions (base scope + extras/change orders)
  • Which codes are labor, which are equipment, which are materials
  • Example: "3100 = Backfill, 3200 = Excavation, 3300 = Concrete..."

2 Sample Documents (1 week's worth)

  • Field Progress Reports (daily)
  • LEM Sheets (extra work tracking)
  • Crew Timesheets (weekly payroll hours)
  • Equipment Safety Checklists (daily pre-use inspections)
  • A mechanic invoice or service record
  • A material/vendor invoice

These samples configure the document extraction templates for your specific form layouts.

3 Crew Roster

  • Names of all field staff (as they write them on reports — nicknames count)
  • Role for each: operator, labourer, foreman, superintendent
  • Which crews are on which projects

4 Equipment Fleet List

  • Unit number (your internal ID), make, model, year
  • Current hour meter reading (approximate is fine)
  • Last known service date and hours for each unit
  • Any active rental equipment

5 Maintenance Interval Specs

  • Manufacturer maintenance schedules per equipment model
  • Or your mechanic's recommended intervals if they differ
  • Last completed service records per unit (if available)

6 Active Projects

  • Project name, number, location
  • Which crew and equipment are assigned to each
  • Budget per project (PM or owner only — used for cost tracking setup)

7 Rate Tables (PM or owner only)

  • Labor rates by role (billing rate or payroll rate — whichever you track against)
  • Equipment rates per unit (internal charge-out rates)

These are visible only to users with Project Manager access — field staff never see dollar amounts.

8 Roles & Access

Who needs access and at what level? Provide name and email for each person.

Role What They See What They Do
Project Controls Hours, quantities, equipment usage — NO costs Upload docs, validate data, flag discrepancies
Project Manager Everything + costs, budgets, profit/loss Full visibility, approvals, cost decisions
Controller / Accounting Invoices, material costs — NOT full project P&L Submit vendor/material invoices
Foreman (optional) Their crew and project only Upload daily reports, confirm flagged items

9 Vendor List (optional but helpful)

  • Key vendors and suppliers (mechanics, material suppliers, rental companies)
  • Contact info and typical services

Helps the system automatically match and categorize future invoices.

10 Existing Cost Control Spreadsheet

  • A blank template of how you currently track project costs

The system output will match your existing format so there's no learning curve.

11 AI Access Setup

Important: The platform uses AI models to process documents, run agents, and generate reports. You'll need an API key — this is separate from any personal AI subscription you may have.
  • Create an OpenRouter account at openrouter.ai and add a $5-20 deposit
  • Typical monthly cost: $5-50 depending on usage
Your Situation What You Need
No existing AI subscription OpenRouter API key (recommended)
Already have Claude Max / ChatGPT Pro Still need a separate API key — personal plans don't cover platform use
Compliance requirements Direct provider API key with BAA, or self-hosted model
Data must never leave your servers Custom LLM endpoint (we'll configure during onboarding)

What Happens at the Onboarding Session

  1. We walk through your sample documents together and configure the extraction templates
  2. Set up your cost code structure, crew roster, and equipment fleet
  3. Configure maintenance intervals and load service history
  4. Set up user accounts with appropriate access levels
  5. Run a test: upload one field report and verify the extracted data matches reality
  6. Train your team on the upload workflow

Typical onboarding: 1-2 sessions, 2-4 hours total.
