4. Data Processing and AI Disclosure

Key disclosure: When you use this Platform, your business data is sent to third-party AI companies for processing. This happens regardless of where the Platform itself is hosted. The sections below explain exactly what data goes where and how you can control it.

4.1 What Data Is Processed by LLMs

The following types of data may be sent to LLM providers during normal Platform operation:

  • Email content submitted for triage, classification, or response drafting
  • Documents uploaded for analysis (PDFs, images, text files)
  • Instructions and queries sent to agents via Discord or the API
  • Business data referenced during agent operations (watchlist items, trade history, contact information)
  • Generated documents, reports, and proposals during creation

4.2 Service Tiers and Data Flow

The Platform offers three deployment tiers with different data flow characteristics:

TierData FlowData Leaves Your Control?

SaaS / Dedicated tier: Data flows through the following path:

  1. Your input → Platform server (processes and prepares the request)
  2. Platform server → OpenRouter API (US-based routing service that directs requests to LLM providers)
  3. OpenRouter → LLM Provider API (the AI company that processes your text and generates a response)
  4. LLM response → Platform server → You

At minimum, your data passes through three jurisdictions: your location, the OpenRouter intermediary (United States), and the LLM provider's infrastructure.

Private Cloud and Enterprise tiers: Your Organization configures a custom LLM endpoint hosted on infrastructure you control. Data flows directly from the Platform to your LLM server without passing through OpenRouter or any third-party provider. The Platform Owner can deploy a private LLM (such as Llama, Mistral, or Qwen) on GPU servers in the jurisdiction of your choice.

4.4 What We Do Before Data Reaches LLMs

The Platform applies automatic protections before sending data to LLM providers:

  • Prompt injection defense: All user-supplied text is scanned and filtered for patterns that could manipulate AI agents.
  • Sensitive data redaction: Passwords, API keys, authentication tokens, credit card numbers, bank account numbers, and government ID numbers are automatically stripped from content before it reaches any LLM provider.
  • Configurable redaction: Organizations can set their redaction level to "strict" (also removes phone numbers, addresses, dates of birth) or "moderate" (credentials and financial data only).
  • Email folder scoping: Organizations can restrict which email folders agents may access and block specific senders from agent processing.

4.5 What We Cannot Control

Once data is sent to an LLM provider's API:

  • The provider processes it on their infrastructure under their own terms of service
  • Providers may temporarily log or cache data for abuse monitoring or debugging
  • We cannot verify what happens to data inside the provider's systems
  • Data processed by a provider cannot be "recalled" or deleted from their logs after the fact

We select providers that commit to not training on customer data (Anthropic, OpenAI via API). However, their policies may change without our control. Organizations should review provider terms independently.

4.6 Platform Infrastructure

The Platform's own infrastructure (database, file storage, application servers) is currently hosted in the United States. This means your stored data (emails, documents, settings, trade history) resides on US-based servers even when not being processed by LLMs. The Platform Owner may migrate infrastructure to other jurisdictions at their discretion.

5. Data Sovereignty

5.1 SaaS / Dedicated Tier

On the SaaS and Dedicated tiers, your data is processed by third-party LLM providers in jurisdictions outside your control. There is no configuration on these tiers that guarantees data stays within a specific country.

5.2 Private Cloud and Enterprise Tiers

On the Private Cloud and Enterprise tiers, full data sovereignty is achievable. The Platform supports custom LLM endpoints, allowing your Organization to:

  • Deploy open-source AI models (Llama, Mistral, Qwen, etc.) on GPU servers in the jurisdiction of your choice
  • Configure the Platform to route all AI processing to your self-hosted endpoint
  • Verify via the Platform's health check and routing APIs that no data flows to third-party providers
  • Deploy the entire Platform stack on your own infrastructure for full air-gapped operation

The Platform Owner maintains a GPU Hosting Playbook covering compliant deployment options in Canada, United States, European Union, United Kingdom, Australia, Middle East, and Asia Pacific.
